    Exploring the Pokemon dataset with pandas and seaborn

    Redwan Huq

    2017-01-19 10:00

    Comments

    The Pokemon dataset is a complete listing of all Pokemon species as of mid-2016, and contains detailed information about their type and statistics. Considering their diversity, it's worthwhile analyzing the datset to identify any insights regarding the design of new Pokemon and to potentially assist players in selecting the best Pokemon. And having been a huge Pokemon fan as a kid, I'm also dreadfully curious.

    While we could easily analyze the dataset using Excel, this software isn't very handy for cleaning messy datasets or telling a story. In addition, Excel is proprietary, not capable of processing large data sets (limited to 1,048,576 rows), and its analyses lack repeatability and transparency. One solution is to use Jupyter notebooks for exploratory analysis combined with Python's renowned open-source libraries: pandas for data analysis and seaborn for high-level visualizations.

    The Pokemon dataset was made available on Kaggle here. Let's begin by loading it and taking a peek inside.
